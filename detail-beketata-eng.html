BEKET ATA MOSQUE

A sacred site of worship and historical legacy in Mangystau

The Beket Ata Mosque is an underground architectural and religious monument of the 18th century located in the Oglandy valley on the Mangyshlak Peninsula. It is a sacred place for Muslims and pilgrims from across Kazakhstan.

  • Founded: around 1750
  • Architecture: underground halls carved into the rock
  • Significance: a site of pilgrimage and spiritual reflection
