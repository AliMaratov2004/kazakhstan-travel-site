SEMEY WOODEN MOSQUE

A unique example of Islamic wooden architecture

The Semey Wooden Mosque (also known as the Agash Mosque) is one of Kazakhstan's oldest religious landmarks. Built in the late 19th century, it was constructed entirely of wood without using a single nail.

  • Year built: 1893
  • Materials: larch, pine
  • Features: carved minaret, wooden decorations, national ornaments

What to see?

  • 🕌 Unique 19th-century architecture
  • 🧱 Historical atmosphere of wood craftsmanship
  • 📷 Great spot for photography

How to get there?

📍 Semey, Auezov Street
🚕 Accessible by taxi or public transport

Information:

🕋 Currently functioning as an active mosque
💵 Free entry

The Semey Wooden Mosque is not only a place of worship, but also a historical treasure of the city. 🌿
