NUR-ALEM

NUR-ALEM Museum of Future Energy

Nur Alem is a unique museum and architectural landmark of Kazakhstan, built specifically for the EXPO-2017 International Exhibition in Astana. It is the largest spherical building in the world, representing innovation, sustainable development, and the future of energy.

  • Year built: 2017
  • Height: 100 meters
  • Diameter: 80 meters
  • Materials: glass, metal
  • Architects: Adrian Smith and Gordon Gill

What to see?

The museum has 8 floors, each dedicated to different sources of future energy:
✅ Solar Energy ☀
✅ Wind Energy 💨
✅ Water Energy 💦
✅ Biomass Energy 🌱
✅ Space Energy 🚀
✅ Nuclear Fusion ⚛
✅ Science and Innovation 🔬
✅ The Future of Kazakhstan 🇰🇿

On the top floor, there is a panoramic observation deck with stunning views of Astana.

Location:

📍 Address: 55/5 Mangilik El Avenue, Astana, Kazakhstan
🚖 Buses: 53, 54, 12, 51; taxi or on foot

Entrance fee:

💰 Adults: 1500 ₸
💰 Children (under 15): 800 ₸
💰 Free: children under 6, pensioners, and people with disabilities
