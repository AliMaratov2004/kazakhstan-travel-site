ALZHIR

ALZHIR – Memorial Complex for Victims of Political Repressions

ALZHIR (Akmola Camp for Wives of Traitors to the Motherland) is one of the most tragic symbols of Stalin’s repressions in Kazakhstan’s history. The memorial complex stands on the site of the former camp where thousands of innocent women were imprisoned. Today, it serves as a powerful reminder of remembrance, sorrow, and human resilience.

  • Year of opening: 2007
  • Location: Akmol village, near Astana
  • Main features: museum, “Arch of Sorrow” monument, exhibition halls

What to See?

  • 🕊 Museum with documents, photos, and artifacts
  • ⛓ “Arch of Sorrow” – a symbol of suffering and hope
  • 🎥 Interactive exhibits, archive footage, documentaries

How to Get There?

📍 Address: Akmol village, 40 km from Astana
🚗 By car: 30–40 minutes via Astana–Karaganda highway
🚌 Guided tours available from Astana

Prices:

💰 Entry: 500 KZT (adult)
💰 Free for students and privileged categories
💰 Guided tour: from 2,000 KZT

ALZHIR is a place where memory of the past calls for peace, justice, and humanity. 🕊🕯
