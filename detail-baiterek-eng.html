BAITEREK

Baiterek – a symbol of Kazakhstan’s independence

Baiterek is one of the main landmarks of Astana and a symbol of the new era of independent Kazakhstan. The monument represents an ancient Kazakh legend about the Tree of Life and the sacred bird Samruk, which lays a golden egg on the top of the tree.

  • Year built: 2002
  • Height: 105 meters (the observation deck is at 97 meters — symbolizing the year 1997, when the capital moved to Astana)
  • Materials: metal, glass, concrete
  • Architect: Akmurza Rustembekov

What to see?

At the top of Baiterek there is an observation deck with a panoramic view of Astana. Inside the sphere is a golden handprint of Kazakhstan's First President — Nursultan Nazarbayev, where tourists traditionally place their hand and make a wish.

Location:

📍 Address: 14 Nurzhol Boulevard, Yesil District, Astana, Kazakhstan
🚖 Buses: No. 10, 12, 18, 21

Entrance fee:

💰 Adults: 2000 ₸
💰 Children: 1000 ₸
💰 Free entry: children under 5, seniors, people with disabilities
