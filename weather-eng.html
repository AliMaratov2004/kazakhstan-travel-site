Weather in Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan’s remoteness from the oceans and vast territory influence its climate. As a result, the country’s nature is impressively diverse. The climate is sharply continental (except the south), with average temperatures ranging from –4ºC to –19ºC in January, and +19ºC to +26ºC in July. Winters can reach –45ºC and summers can heat up to +30ºC. The country clearly experiences all four seasons — frosty winter, blossoming spring, hot summer, and golden autumn.

AUTUMN

🍂 Autumn — a season of cozy journeys and picturesque views
The perfect time to enjoy nature’s calmness, go hiking in the mountains, and take historical excursions.

🍁 Markakol — the emerald lake of the Altai region, becomes especially beautiful in autumn, surrounded by golden pine trees.
🏜 Bektau-Ata — a unique natural reserve with rocky labyrinths and mysterious caves. In autumn, the weather is comfortably cool.
🍷 Zharkent Vineyards — grape harvest season, offering wine tasting and tours of local wineries.
🏞 Tamgaly-Tas Gorge — ancient rock carvings and the peaceful banks of the Ili River make it a perfect destination for autumn travel.

🍁 Kostanay — ideal for an autumn stroll in Victory Park, a visit to the Tobyl Region Museum, or grabbing fresh baked goods at the Central Food Market on a crisp day.
🏛 Pavlodar — a great season to explore history: Bukhar Zhyrau’s House-Museum, the Regional History Museum, and the scenic Irtysh River embankment are especially charming in autumn.
🍷 Semey — visit the Abai Literary Memorial Museum and enjoy local wines in the city’s restaurants.
🎨 Kokshetau — a peaceful and inspiring city for autumn walks. Stroll through the Central Park and explore the “Kokshetau” Art Gallery.

WINTER

❄️ Winter — a time of snow-covered magical landscapes
In winter, Kazakhstan turns into a fairytale land. It's the best season for skiing, winter festivals, and admiring snow-capped mountains.

⛷ Shymbulak — the most popular ski resort located near Almaty.
🏔 Burabay — looks like a fairytale in winter.
🏞 Kolsai Lakes — freeze in winter, revealing the snowy slopes of the Tian Shan mountains.
🧊 Astana — a city of winter celebrations, ice sculptures, and festive lights.
🌨 Oskemen — a quiet city with cozy mountain resorts ideal for family vacations.

SPRING

🌸 Spring — the season of nature's awakening
During this time, the steppe blooms and the weather becomes perfect for travel.

🌷 The tulip fields of Mangystau — bright flowers bloom creating a breathtaking view.
🏔 Turgen Gorge — known for its waterfalls and blossoming gardens.
🕌 Otyrar and Sauran — ancient cities with rich historical heritage.
🏞 Tengiz-Korgalzhyn Nature Reserve — thousands of pink flamingos arrive in spring.

🏙 Almaty – in spring, Sakura and tulips bloom in the First President's Park, and you can enjoy panoramic views from Kok Tobe hill.
🏛 Astana (Nur-Sultan) – spring is a great time to visit the National Museum of Kazakhstan, the Palace of Peace and Reconciliation, and the Astana Opera Theatre.
🚋 Shymkent – the old town comes alive in spring with a unique atmosphere. Walk through the Central Park, enjoy the pedestrian Arbat street, and visit the ethnographic museum.
🎭 Karaganda – perfect for a cultural day out: explore the Museum of Political Repression in Dolinka, the Musical Comedy Theatre, and the Miners' Glory Museum.

SUMMER

☀ Summer — the season of adventure and active recreation
This is the best time for mountain trips and relaxing by the water.

🏝 Caspian Sea (Aktau, Kendirli) — sandy beaches and warm waters.
🏜 Bozzhyra (Mangystau) — surreal landscapes and breathtaking sunsets.
🌊 Alakol — a warm lake known for its healing properties.
🏕 Katon-Karagay National Park — ideal for nature and adventure lovers.

🏖 Aktau – one of the best places for summer vacation. Spend your day on the city’s Caspian beaches, then stroll along the promenade and enjoy the sunset in the evening.
🎡 Astana – in summer, ride the “Ailand” Ferris wheel, relax at the “Alau” water park, and unwind along the Esil river embankment.
🏊‍♂ Taraz – visit Zhambyl Park and cool off at the “Keruen” water park or one of the outdoor pools on hot days.
🎭 Oral (Uralsk) – admire the historic architecture, walk through Kirov Park, and visit the regional drama theatre or Pushkin Museum.
