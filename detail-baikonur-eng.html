BAIKONUR COSMODROME

Baikonur – the world's first spaceport

The Baikonur Cosmodrome is a legendary site where the history of human spaceflight began. It was from here that the first artificial satellite and the first human in space, Yuri Gagarin, were launched.

  • Established: 1955
  • Area: over 6,700 km²
  • Launches: more than 1500 space launches
  • Current Use: Operated jointly by Russia and Kazakhstan

What can you see?

  • 🚀 Launch pads with real rockets
  • 🛰️ Space exploration museum
  • 📸 Monument to Gagarin and "Avenue of Space Heroes"

How to get there?

📍 Kyzylorda Region, Baikonur City
✈️ Fly to Kyzylorda, then drive or take a train
📋 Advance registration required (especially for foreign citizens)

Interesting facts

🌍 Baikonur is the largest operational spaceport in the world
📅 Each launch is a major event attracting tourists and media
🎫 Space tours available with guided visits and accommodations

Baikonur is not just a location — it's a living story of humanity's reach for the stars, inspiring curiosity and exploration!
