BURABAY

Burabay – "The Switzerland of Kazakhstan"

Burabay is a unique natural reserve in the Akmola region, known for its picturesque lakes, mountainous landscapes, and dense forests. It is one of Kazakhstan’s top tourist destinations, perfect for relaxation, hiking, and wellness retreats.

  • Location: Akmola Region, 250 km from Astana
  • Area: approximately 129,000 hectares
  • Status: National Park (since 2000)

What to See?

  • 🌽 Lake Burabay – the main natural attraction
  • 🗻 Okzhetpes Rock – the legendary cliff
  • ⛰ Mount Kokshe – scenic peak with panoramic views
  • 🚣 Bolshoye Chebachye Lake – perfect for swimming and fishing

What to Do?

  • 🚶‍♂️ Trekking and hiking – routes through mountains and forests
  • 🚣 Boating and catamarans – explore the lakes
  • 🎕 Camping and picnics – relax in nature
  • 🏡 Visit ethnovillages and spa resorts – wellness activities

How to Get There?

📍 Burabay village, Akmola region
🚆 Train: 3–4 hours from Astana to "Kurort-Borovoye" station
🚗 By car: 3 hours via Astana – Shchuchinsk highway
🚌 Buses: depart from Astana bus stations

Hours and Prices

🕒 National Park: open year-round
💰 Entry fee: 500 KZT
💰 Boat ride: from 2,000 KZT

Burabay is a natural paradise of Kazakhstan that everyone should visit! ⛰️🌾🌽
