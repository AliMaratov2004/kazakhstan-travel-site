KAINDY LAKE

Kaindy Lake – the Sunken Forest of Kazakhstan

Kaindy Lake is a unique mountain lake famous for its submerged spruce trees rising from the water. It was formed after an earthquake in 1911, when a landslide blocked the river and flooded part of the forest. Thanks to the crystal-clear water, the tree trunks are clearly visible underwater, creating a mystical atmosphere.

  • Altitude: 1,667 m above sea level
  • Depth: up to 30 m
  • Water Temperature: +4…+8°C even in summer
  • Water Color: Emerald blue

Activities

  • 🏞 Hiking and photo sessions – breathtaking views of the lake and mountains
  • 🚣‍♂ Boat rides – exploring the sunken forest
  • 🎣 Fishing – trout (catch and release)
  • 🪶 Trekking – forest and mountain trails

How to get there?

📍 Almaty Region, 300 km from Almaty
🚗 By car: 5–6 hours via the Almaty – Saty route, then 5 km on foot
🚌 Tours available from Almaty

Prices

💰 National park entrance: 1,000 KZT per person
💰 Boat rental: from 3,000 KZT

Kaindy Lake is a one-of-a-kind natural wonder you must see with your own eyes! 🌲🏜✨
