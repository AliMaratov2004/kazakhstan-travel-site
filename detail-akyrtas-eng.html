AKYRTAS

Akyrtas – a mysterious ancient palace-temple complex

Akyrtas is an archaeological site that represents an 8th-century palace complex located near Taraz. Its exact origin and function are still debated by historians. Its unique layout and architecture make it one of the most enigmatic places in Kazakhstan.

  • Construction period: 8th century
  • Material: red stone (sandstone)
  • Purpose: palace or temple (not definitively known)
  • Status: active archaeological excavation site

Interesting Facts

  • 🏛 Monumental walls up to 3 meters thick
  • 🌀 Multi-level water supply system
  • 🔍 Often referred to as the "Kazakh Stonehenge"

How to get there?

📍 Zhambyl Region, 40 km from Taraz
🚗 By car via the Taraz – Aksholak highway
🚌 Minibuses or taxis from Taraz

Useful Information

⏰ Open at any time
🎟️ Entry: free
📷 Great spot for photography and archaeological tours

Akyrtas is a place where the past comes alive, immersing visitors in the spirit of the ancient East!
