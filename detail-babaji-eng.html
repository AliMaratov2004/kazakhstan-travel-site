BABAJI-KHATUN MAUSOLEUM

A historical 9th–10th century mausoleum – a symbol of female spirituality

The Babaji-Khatun Mausoleum is located near the Aisha-Bibi Mausoleum and stands as a remarkable example of medieval architecture. Built in the 9th–10th centuries, it has become a symbol of female wisdom and spiritual grace.

  • Period: 9th–10th centuries
  • Architecture: Domed structure made of fired bricks
  • Feature: Unique stalactite-style dome
  • Purpose: A mausoleum in honor of a righteous woman

What to see?

  • 🏛 Unique dome based on a 16-sided foundation
  • 🧱 Ancient brickwork and ornamentation
  • 📸 A historic site ideal for photography

How to get there?

📍 Zhambyl Region, next to Aisha-Bibi Mausoleum
🚗 By car – approx. 20 minutes from Taraz city center
🚌 Regular guided tours available from Taraz

Visitor Information

⏰ Open daily from 09:00 to 19:00
🎟️ Entry: free
📷 Photo and video shooting allowed

The Babaji-Khatun Mausoleum is not only an architectural gem but also a tribute to the spiritual and intellectual strength of women.
